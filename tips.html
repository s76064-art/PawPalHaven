Pet Care Tips

Expert advice to keep your best friends happy and healthy. Click each section to explore videos and guides.

Balanced Diet

Cats are obligate carnivores and need high-quality animal protein.

  • Feed age-appropriate wet and dry food.
  • Avoid cow's milk (most cats are lactose intolerant).
  • Always provide a clean, moving water source.

Litter Box Best Practices

Hygiene is critical for feline mental health.

  • Scoop daily and deep clean weekly.
  • Place boxes in low-traffic, quiet areas.

Grooming & Coat Care

Regular brushing helps reduce shedding and prevents hairballs. Short-haired cats should be brushed weekly, while long-haired cats need daily grooming to avoid matting.

Play & Mental Stimulation

Cats need daily playtime to stay active and prevent boredom. Interactive toys such as feather wands or laser pointers help stimulate their natural hunting instincts.

Expert Tip

Cats spend 12-16 hours a day sleeping! Ensure their resting area is warm and elevated away from noise.

Physical & Mental Exercise

Active dogs are happy dogs! Regular movement prevents obesity and destructive behavior.

  • Walk at least 30-60 minutes daily.
  • Use puzzle toys to keep their brains sharp.

Preventative Health

  • Monthly flea and heartworm prevention is vital.
  • Brush teeth weekly to prevent dental disease.

Proper Nutrition

Feed dogs a balanced diet based on their age, size, and activity level. Avoid giving human food that may be harmful, such as chocolate, onions, or overly salty foods.

Grooming & Hygiene

Regular grooming keeps dogs clean and comfortable. This includes brushing fur, trimming nails, cleaning ears, and bathing when necessary to prevent skin problems.

Cage & Environment

Birds need space to stretch wings and fly short distances. Choose a cage at least twice their wingspan. Place perches of varying diameters to prevent arthritis.

Diet & Socialization

Provide high-quality pellets, fresh fruits, and vegetables. Birds are intelligent; spend 30 minutes daily interacting to prevent boredom.

Grooming & Feather Care

Birds naturally groom themselves, but providing clean water for bathing helps maintain healthy feathers and skin, which are essential for flight and insulation.

Mental Stimulation & Toys

Birds are intelligent animals that require mental stimulation. Providing a variety of toys and rotating them regularly helps prevent boredom and destructive behavior.

Environment

Avoid placing cages in direct sunlight or drafty areas. Clean cages weekly to prevent bacteria.

OIn addition to pellets and fruits, occasionally provide natural treats like millet sprays or cuttlebone to ensure calcium and trace nutrient intake.

Proper Diet & Digestion

Rabbits have sensitive digestive systems and require a high-fiber diet to stay healthy.

  • Provide unlimited fresh hay (timothy or orchard hay).
  • Offer leafy greens such as romaine lettuce and herbs.
  • Avoid sugary treats and processed food.

Daily Handling & Bonding

Spend a few minutes daily gently handling your rabbit to build trust. This reduces stress and helps them become comfortable with grooming and vet visits.

Expert Tip

Rabbits need chew toys to prevent overgrown teeth. Always provide safe wooden toys or hay blocks.
