Birds ‚Äì Complete Care Guide

1. Cage & Environment:

Birds need space to stretch wings and fly short distances. Choose a cage that is at least twice the wingspan of your bird in every direction. Place perches of varying diameters to exercise their feet and prevent arthritis. Avoid placing cages in direct sunlight or drafty areas.

2. Diet & Nutrition:

A varied diet keeps birds healthy. Include high-quality pellets, fresh fruits (apple, berries, kiwi), vegetables (carrots, spinach, broccoli), and seeds in moderation. Avoid avocado, chocolate, caffeine, alcohol, and salty foods‚Äîthey are toxic. Provide fresh water daily.

3. Mental Stimulation & Socialization:

Birds are intelligent and can get bored. Offer toys, mirrors, bells, swings, and puzzle feeders. Spend at least 30 minutes daily interacting for social species like parrots. Rotate toys to keep them interesting.

4. Hygiene & Health:

Clean the cage weekly, and sanitize food and water dishes daily. Watch for changes in droppings, feather condition, appetite, or behavior, as birds often hide illness until it becomes serious. Schedule regular vet checkups with an avian vet.

5. Exercise:

Allow supervised out-of-cage time in a safe area to fly, stretch, and explore. This prevents obesity and supports mental well-being.
